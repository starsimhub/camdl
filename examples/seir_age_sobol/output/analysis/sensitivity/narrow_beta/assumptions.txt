Sensitivity analysis assumptions:

Methodology:
  Sobol first-order and total-order indices (Saltelli et al. 2010)
  Bootstrap confidence intervals (1000 resamples, 95% level)

Sampling:
  Quasi-random (Halton sequence) over specified parameter ranges
  Output variance decomposition is CONDITIONAL on these bounds
  Different bounds (designs) produce different indices

Design "narrow_beta":
  Base sample size: n = 256
  Parameters: k = 3
  Total points: n(2k+2) = 1280

Cross-design VOI interpretation:
  Comparing sensitivity indices across designs estimates the VALUE of
  narrowing parameter uncertainty. This is a sensitivity landscape
  comparison, NOT a Bayesian posterior. The magnitude depends on the
  assumed ranges. For prior-weighted analysis, use parameter_points.tsv
  and outputs.tsv with importance weights.
