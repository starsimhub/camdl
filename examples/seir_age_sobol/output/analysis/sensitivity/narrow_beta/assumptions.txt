Sensitivity analysis assumptions:

Methodology:
  Sobol first-order and total-order indices (Saltelli et al. 2010)
  Bootstrap confidence intervals (1000 resamples, 95% level)

Sampling:
  Quasi-random (Halton sequence) over specified parameter ranges
  Output variance decomposition is CONDITIONAL on these bounds
  Different bounds (designs) produce different indices

Design "narrow_beta":
  Base sample size: n = 1024
  Parameters: k = 3
  Total points: n(2k+2) = 5120

Results summary:
  narrow_beta | peak_I_child | beta: S1=0.001 ST=0.003
  narrow_beta | peak_I_child | gamma: S1=0.617 ST=0.613
  narrow_beta | peak_I_child | sigma: S1=0.372 ST=0.386
  narrow_beta | peak_I_adult | beta: S1=0.001 ST=0.004
  narrow_beta | peak_I_adult | gamma: S1=0.630 ST=0.627
  narrow_beta | peak_I_adult | sigma: S1=0.357 ST=0.373

Cross-design comparison:
  Comparing sensitivity indices across designs shows how the model's
  sensitivity landscape shifts as parameter uncertainty narrows.
