VOI analysis assumptions:

Method:
  Preposterior importance sampling (Raiffa & Schlaifer 1961;
  Strong et al. 2014 for health economics)

Prior:
  N = 2048 samples from design 'prior'

Decision:
  Actions: no_sia, target_10, sia_all
  Reference: no_sia
  Utility: total_cases (minimize)
  Costs (utility units subtracted per action):
    sia_all: 78000
    target_10: 36000

Monte Carlo draws per study configuration: M = 1000

Studies:
  es: binomial(p_positive), sizes/sds: 5, 10, 20, 30, 50

ESS WARNINGS (importance sampling may be unreliable):
  es n=5: min ESS = 309
  es n=10: min ESS = 209
  es n=20: min ESS = 144
  es n=30: min ESS = 97
  es n=50: min ESS = 54

Limitations:
  - Each study is assumed to inform exactly one parameter
  - Prior is approximated by the design sample, not an analytic form
  - Importance sampling degrades when the study is very informative
